Haven Product Strategy v2.1

1. Executive Summary

Haven is building a world-class relationship operating system for couples.

Its purpose is not merely to provide tools for communication, journaling, or memory keeping. Its purpose is to help couples understand each other more deeply, communicate more clearly, preserve what matters, navigate tension more wisely, repair more safely, and grow together over time.

Haven should become a product that feels:
	•	deeply human
	•	emotionally intelligent
	•	premium
	•	calm
	•	warm
	•	elegant
	•	trustworthy
	•	world-class

In the near term, Haven’s strategy is to strengthen its core product loop, close the most important product gaps, and transform its most valuable surfaces into beautiful, high-retention experiences.

In the longer term, Haven should evolve beyond a collection of features into a true relationship intelligence system: a product that helps couples reflect, communicate, remember, repair, and build a shared life with greater depth and care.

Haven Mission

To help couples build deeper understanding, intimacy, and lasting connection.

Haven Vision

A world where deep, loving relationships are easier to build, nurture, and sustain.

Haven Goal

To build software that helps couples turn everyday communication into deeper understanding, greater intimacy, and lasting connection.

3. Product Thesis

Deep relationships do not become stronger simply because people care. They become stronger when people are supported in remembering, expressing, understanding, and acting with care over time.

Most relationship pain does not come only from lack of love. It often comes from:
	•	misunderstanding
	•	emotional miscommunication
	•	forgotten context
	•	unspoken needs
	•	lack of shared memory
	•	lack of structure for healthy repair
	•	lack of support during difficult conversations
	•	the inability to turn daily life into long-term relational growth

Haven exists to solve this.

Haven should help couples:
	•	communicate better
	•	understand each other more deeply
	•	make daily emotional life easier to navigate
	•	preserve meaningful memories
	•	build a shared understanding of each other
	•	create intentional futures together
	•	repair more wisely when things become difficult

5.2 Trust and Correctness Gates

Before Haven advances deeper into higher-order product work such as richer AI support, relationship intelligence, guided repair, or premium editorial refinement, it must first satisfy a set of non-negotiable trust and correctness gates.

These are not merely implementation details. They are foundational to emotional trust, product credibility, privacy safety, and user willingness to rely on Haven in meaningful relationship moments.

A. Draft-first writing must be reliable
Journal writing must default to safe draft behavior.

Users must be able to:
	•	write freely
	•	pause safely
	•	return later
	•	revise repeatedly
	•	trust that unfinished writing is not prematurely transformed or interpreted

Draft saving must be stable, interruption-free, and predictable.

Autosave and manual save must preserve user work without surprising state changes, content loss, or hidden downstream actions.

B. AI participation must be explicit, bounded, and user-invoked
Saving content must not automatically trigger AI analysis.

AI should enter the Journal flow only when a user explicitly chooses to invoke it through a clear action such as AI 分析.

This protects:
	•	user agency
	•	emotional safety
	•	privacy boundaries
	•	trust in Haven’s writing experience

Users must always understand:
	•	whether content is still a private draft
	•	whether it has been shared
	•	whether AI has processed it
	•	what version of content AI has seen

C. Media must be rendered correctly and treated appropriately
Images inserted into journals must:
	•	render correctly in Journal surfaces
	•	render correctly in Home previews where applicable
	•	remain part of the user-authored experience

Images should not be casually collapsed into placeholder text such as “圖片” when the product should instead show meaningful visual content.

If AI analysis is performed, media handling must be clearly bounded.

The default expectation should be that attachments are treated as presentation and authored context unless a deliberately designed and consent-based analysis mode says otherwise.

Media should not be included in AI interpretation by default unless users clearly understand and explicitly consent to that mode.

D. Socially meaningful surfaces must preserve authorship clarity
Whenever Haven shows user-generated relationship content on shared or semi-shared surfaces, authorship and directionality must be clear.

For example, appreciation notes should communicate:
	•	who wrote the note
	•	who it is for
	•	when it was written
	•	how it fits into the shared relational context

Haven must avoid emotionally ambiguous presentation patterns that obscure authorship or create avoidable confusion.

E. Correctness comes before expressive expansion
Before Haven expands Journal into a deeper premium writing system, before it evolves AI analysis into a richer insight layer, and before it pushes further into relationship intelligence, the existing product must first be trustworthy in its core behaviors.

This means Haven should not tolerate foundational issues such as:
	•	unstable autosave
	•	content loss during save or hydration
	•	hidden AI triggering
	•	broken media rendering
	•	ambiguous authorship
	•	inconsistent read/write state transitions

Strategic implication
These trust and correctness gates are prerequisites for strategic progress, not separate from it.

If Haven fails these foundations, users will not fully trust:
	•	journaling as a reflective space
	•	AI as a bounded and humane assistant
	•	shared surfaces as reliable relationship memory
	•	the product as a premium and emotionally safe environment

If Haven satisfies these foundations, it creates the conditions necessary for:
	•	richer Journal evolution
	•	stronger AI participation
	•	better shared memory systems
	•	deeper relationship intelligence
	•	more defensible retention and trust over time

Execution principle
For product planning purposes, these trust and correctness gates should be treated as P0 enabling work.

They should be completed before or alongside the earliest phase of higher-order product investments in:
	•	Journal redesign
	•	AI reflection and analysis
	•	Love Map / relationship intelligence
	•	guided repair
	•	premium surface refinement

Haven should not attempt to scale emotional depth, AI sophistication, or product complexity on top of unstable foundations.

6. Core Product Principles

6.1 Emotional intelligence over raw utility

Haven should not merely expose controls or store data.

It should help users understand themselves and each other more clearly.

6.2 Beauty should deepen trust

Beauty is not decoration.

Design should create calm, safety, intimacy, clarity, and confidence.

6.3 AI should support, not dominate

AI should help translate, reflect, summarize, clarify, and surface insight.

It should not remove user agency, replace human meaning, or take over the relationship.

6.4 Relationship data should become living shared knowledge

Haven should evolve from isolated features into a connected relationship knowledge system.

6.5 Depth should be intentional

Users should be able to choose whether they want lighter, warmer, or deeper interaction modes.

Emotional depth should be supported, not forced.

6.6 The product should reward return

Haven should become more valuable the more a couple uses it.

Its memory, insight, and understanding should compound over time.

6.7 Premium applies to every surface

Not only the hero pages, but also:
	•	settings
	•	archives
	•	modals
	•	empty states
	•	forms
	•	onboarding
	•	notifications
	•	repair flows

must feel deeply considered.

6.8 AI participation must be consent-based and bounded

If AI enters a couple’s shared conversation space, it must do so with clear user consent and clear boundaries.

Haven must never casually blur the line between:
	•	private reflection
	•	shared couple context
	•	AI-generated interpretation
	•	human-authored meaning

6.9 Privacy and trust are foundational

Haven must never use private reflections recklessly, especially across partner boundaries.

Private one-person AI conversations and shared couple conversations must remain clearly separated unless explicitly authorized.

6.10 Repair matters more than cleverness

In moments of tension, the goal of AI is not to impress, win, or sound brilliant.

Its goal is to help couples slow down, understand better, and repair more safely.

7. Core Product Loop

Haven’s strongest long-term product loop should look like this:
	1.	A user enters Haven through a daily or emotionally relevant moment.
	2.	They reflect, communicate, or interact through journals, card rituals, syncs, memories, prompts, or guided conversation.
	3.	Haven helps transform those interactions into clearer understanding.
	4.	That understanding becomes part of an evolving shared relationship knowledge system.
	5.	The product becomes more personal, more useful, and more emotionally meaningful over time.
	6.	Users return because Haven helps them love, understand, communicate, repair, and connect better.

This is the compounding loop Haven should optimize.

9. Strategic Direction for Key Product Areas

9.1 Journal

Journal should become a world-class reflective writing experience.

Direction
	•	rich writing experience
	•	Markdown support
	•	image upload
	•	better long-form reading and editing
	•	smoother drafting, saving, revisiting, and sharing
	•	Haven-native emotional and visual identity

Journal should follow a draft-first model by default.

Writing and saving should remain private, stable, and recoverable unless the author explicitly chooses to share content or invoke AI analysis.

Strategic role
Journal is not just a feature.

It is one of Haven’s deepest entry points into emotional honesty and relationship understanding.

Important product upgrade
Journal authors should be able to control partner visibility:
	•	share original content
	•	share only AI-translated content
	•	keep private

If AI translation is used, it should be shown only to the partner, not to the original writer.

This gives Haven a uniquely humane communication layer.

The goal is not to copy Heptabase visually, but to achieve a similarly strong writing experience, especially in Markdown editing, long-form drafting, and media support, while expressing Haven’s own emotional, premium, and relational design language.

9.2 Decks / Card Rituals

Decks should become one of Haven’s strongest retention and intimacy engines.

Direction
	•	beautiful ritual feel
	•	stronger emotional pacing
	•	better anticipation and reveal
	•	stronger category identity
	•	easier re-entry into prior card journeys
	•	depth selection using difficulty_level

User-facing framing
Users should not see technical depth labels.

They should see emotionally meaningful options such as:
	•	Light
	•	Warm
	•	Deep

This should apply in:
	•	deck library
	•	home daily empathy surface

Love Map should evolve into a richer shared relationship intelligence system.

Direction
It should contain not only notes, but a structured and living shared knowledge layer about:
	•	identity
	•	preferences
	•	milestones
	•	values
	•	emotional needs
	•	important memories
	•	future plans
	•	shared aspirations

Possible information groups
	•	Identity
	•	Heart
	•	Story
	•	Future

Examples of relationship knowledge
Important dates:
	•	birthdays
	•	relationship anniversary
	•	wedding anniversary

Identity and preferences:
	•	interests
	•	likes and dislikes
	•	love languages
	•	gift and size information
	•	routines and sensitivities

Inner world:
	•	values
	•	worldview
	•	money beliefs
	•	emotional needs
	•	things that matter deeply

Story and memory:
	•	meaningful past experiences
	•	recurring themes in the relationship
	•	unresolved tensions
	•	cherished shared memories

Future:
	•	personal future plans
	•	shared future plans
	•	dreams, goals, and aspirations

Long-term role
This may become one of Haven’s most defensible surfaces.

Naming
“Love Map” may or may not remain the best name.

Possible future naming directions:
	•	Love Map & Blueprint
	•	Relationship Map
	•	Our World
	•	Relationship Atlas

AI update principle
AI should help propose new relationship knowledge and updates based on:
	•	journals
	•	card responses
	•	meaningful conversations
	•	shared memories
	•	other relational signals

But AI should begin as a suggestion layer, not an automatic overwrite layer.

Users should remain in control of what becomes shared relationship truth.

9.4 Memory

Memory should become a premium relationship archive, not just a storage page.

Direction
	•	timeline, calendar, and capsule views should all work beautifully
	•	clicking moments should reliably reveal real content
	•	archive UX should feel emotional, elegant, and alive
	•	Memory should reward long-term usage

Immediate requirement
The calendar bug where users cannot open content from active dates must be fixed.

9.5 Analysis

Analysis should become Haven’s insight center.

Direction
It should answer questions like:
	•	How are we doing lately?
	•	What patterns are emerging?
	•	What needs more attention?
	•	What is getting better?
	•	What might help us connect more deeply?

Important note
Analysis should not remain a dashboard shell.

It needs real product definition, real data flow, and meaningful insight design.

9.6 Mediation and Repair

Repair should be one of Haven’s signature strengths.

Direction
	•	emotionally safe
	•	calm
	•	wise
	•	structured but not robotic
	•	supportive without being preachy

Goal
Mediation and cool-down flows should help couples slow down, reframe, and reconnect more wisely.

Repair should feel like one of Haven’s most trusted and differentiated product capabilities.

9.7 Notifications

Notifications should become a relationship retention system, not a reminder system.

Direction
Notifications should feel:
	•	timely
	•	relevant
	•	warm
	•	relational
	•	non-intrusive

They should guide users back into meaningful loops:
	•	daily sync
	•	journaling
	•	appreciation
	•	card rituals
	•	repair moments
	•	milestone memories

9.8 Settings

Settings should feel like a trust and customization center, not a utility page.

Direction
	•	calm
	•	premium
	•	relationship-aware
	•	emotionally intelligent
	•	clear in meaning and consequence

Settings should help users shape how Haven supports their relationship.

9.9 AI Companion and AI Mediator

Haven should develop an AI-supported conversation layer that helps users communicate with greater clarity, safety, and care.

This should not be treated as a generic chatbot feature.

It should become a deeply integrated relational support system with two primary modes:

A. Private Reflection Companion
A one-on-one AI conversation space where one partner can privately:
	•	clarify emotions
	•	untangle needs
	•	prepare for difficult conversations
	•	translate reactive language into more constructive language
	•	reflect before speaking

This mode should feel safe, supportive, and non-judgmental.

It should help users prepare to communicate better, not become dependent on AI.

B. Shared AI Mediator
A shared conversation mode where the AI can be invited into a couple’s conversation to help:
	•	slow escalation
	•	summarize both sides clearly
	•	distinguish facts, interpretations, emotions, and needs
	•	reduce misunderstanding
	•	guide repair more safely

This mode should not feel like a referee, judge, or lecturer.

It should feel like a calm, wise, non-dominating third presence that helps both people understand each other better.

Product principles for AI Companion / Mediator
The AI conversation layer must be:
	•	consent-based
	•	context-bounded
	•	repair-oriented
	•	emotionally intelligent
	•	privacy-safe
	•	non-dominating
	•	transparent about what context it uses

The AI must never casually reveal one person’s private reflections inside a shared couple conversation.

Private AI reflection and shared AI mediation must remain clearly separated unless explicitly authorized.

Haven may eventually detect signs of escalation and offer a timely invitation into repair mode. However, AI should not aggressively or casually enter shared conversations without clear user understanding and trust boundaries.

Long-term role
If done well, this can become one of Haven’s most distinctive and defensible product capabilities.

It would allow Haven not only to support reflection and memory, but also to support communication in the moment when it matters most.

9.10 Shared Conversation Layer

Haven may eventually require a true shared conversation layer rather than relying only on journals, prompts, and asynchronous surfaces.

This could take one of two forms:
	•	an in-product couple chat experience designed in Haven’s own relational and emotional language
	•	or carefully permissioned integrations with external conversation platforms where technically and ethically feasible

The purpose of this layer would not be to become a generic messaging app.

Its purpose would be to give Haven a living communication surface where reflection, repair, memory, and AI support can operate closer to real relational moments.

Over time, this could become the surface where:
	•	difficult conversations are prepared
	•	misunderstandings are clarified
	•	repair is guided
	•	meaningful moments are preserved
	•	AI mediation can be invited when needed

If Haven ever integrates with third-party messaging platforms, it must do so with extremely high standards for consent, privacy, transparency, and trust.

10. Design Strategy

10.1 Brand and aesthetic direction

Haven should feel:
	•	premium editorial
	•	warm
	•	restrained
	•	modern
	•	intimate
	•	soft but confident
	•	luxurious without being showy

10.2 Theme strategy

Haven may explore two visual theme directions in the near term:
	•	a minimalist premium theme
	•	a warm editorial / magazine-like theme

In the exploration stage, controlled user-facing theme variation may be acceptable if it helps Haven learn which emotional design language creates stronger trust, delight, and retention.

However, Haven should avoid long-term fragmentation into two equally heavy and divergent product systems.

One core design language should ultimately lead, while alternate themes remain disciplined, compatible, and secondary.

10.3 AI interaction design strategy

AI surfaces in Haven must not feel like generic chat utilities.

They should feel:
	•	calm
	•	humane
	•	emotionally safe
	•	clear
	•	warm
	•	premium
	•	trustworthy

In particular, AI conversation and mediation surfaces should avoid:
	•	looking like a generic customer support chat box
	•	feeling noisy or hyperactive
	•	overusing robotic assistant language
	•	dominating the emotional tone of the relationship

11. Integrations Strategy

11.1 External knowledge tools

Long-term, Haven may benefit from integration with:
	•	Notion
	•	possibly Heptabase, if technically possible

But this should happen in stages:
	•	export
	•	one-way sync
	•	selective two-way sync
	•	only later, mature bidirectional sync

Why
Because external sync introduces:
	•	permissions complexity
	•	source-of-truth ambiguity
	•	sync conflicts
	•	schema mapping problems
	•	trust risks

Haven’s internal product value must become strong first.

11.2 AI ecosystem boundaries

Haven should avoid becoming a thin wrapper around general-purpose AI chat.

Its AI capabilities should be differentiated by:
	•	relational context
	•	trust boundaries
	•	repair intelligence
	•	shared memory awareness
	•	careful emotional UX

The goal is not to have “AI features.”

The goal is to build product-native AI that genuinely improves relationships.

12. What Must Be True of Every Feature

Every Haven feature should answer at least one of these questions:
	•	Does this help couples understand each other better?
	•	Does this help couples communicate more clearly?
	•	Does this help couples feel closer?
	•	Does this help preserve or deepen what matters?
	•	Does this reduce friction, confusion, or emotional harm?
	•	Does this make the relationship feel more intentional, more supported, or more alive?

If not, it is likely not core.

14. What Not to Do

To stay strategically strong, Haven should avoid:
	•	adding too many new disconnected features
	•	keeping weak existing structures just because they already exist
	•	over-automating AI updates before users trust the system
	•	splitting design language too early across multiple full themes
	•	prioritizing external integrations before the core product becomes strong
	•	building gamification or analytics deeply before the product meaning is clear
	•	shipping a generic AI chatbot that is not meaningfully relationship-aware
	•	letting AI enter shared couple spaces without explicit trust and consent design
	•	using private user reflections recklessly across partner boundaries
